The 2026 Aircraft:
Koi

Koi is the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute's aircraft for the 2026 AIAA Design/Build/Fly competition. It is heavily optimized to perform the operations of a charter and banner-towing bush plane, maximizing our Ground Mission and Mission 3 scores through lightweight composite design and rapid-integration payloads.

  • Composite Airframe: Features a carbon fiber VARTM monocoque fuselage with a semi-monocoque wood build-up wing, utilizing a circular CFRP spar and PA6-CF 3D-printed wingbox.
  • Banner Mechanism: Deploys a 190-inch Nylon Ripstop banner from beneath the fuselage using a servo-secured rigid gate assembly and an automatic release pin.
  • Charter Payload: Carries form-fitting passenger and cargo molds released sequentially by Tower Pro MG90S servo-actuated bottom hatches to drastically reduce GM times.
  • Propulsion & Gear: Powered by a highly efficient single-tractor electric motor. A taildragger landing gear configuration was chosen to maximize ground clearance for banner stowage while reducing structural weight.
